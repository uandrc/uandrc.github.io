Simplicity

Why it's so great

There's an intrinsic beauty to simplicity. In the world of programming, there's even an acronym for it. KISS (Keep it simple, stupid) was coined by the Department of Defense to remind hardware and software engineers that systems are least error-prone when kept simple. Similarly, "worse is better" states that having simpler, "worse" programs with less functionality can be superior to programs with more user options.

Simplicity is great because it reminds us to focus on the most important task at hand. It is too easy to get caught up in trivial details trying to make something look good, only to neglect the bigger, more important picture. To make something useful and simple is a powerful skill. It is a skill that programmers and non-programmers alike should strive to hone.

By today's standards, the UI of many apps from the past are a little ugly - rough around the edges - sometimes literally, with pixelated buttons and muted colors. Yet, their charm seems to exceed many "beautiful", modern apps. Is some of this charm based solely on nostalgia, as opposed to its artistic merit? Absolutely.

They lack the bells and whistles we are so accustomed to seeing today. Older programs' focus on the core functionality, as opposed to the , makes apps reduce the mental load clients must take on to use the app.

One of the best examples of greatness from simplicity is Apple iMovie's mobile device software. I've tried many editing softwares across several operating systems over several years. Most that I've used have worked well, but it is easy to be overwhelmed by the plethora of options and settings that so frequently plague editing softwares.

However, Apple iMovie focuses on the most important editing functions: cut, crop, simple transitions, text overlay, speed adjustment, audio levels, filters, and voiceover. For most functions, there's only a handful of options that can be changed.

And that is what makes iMovie on mobile so great. It does not have clutter or waste hogging up the screen. iMovie's simplicity lends itself extremely well to being hassle-free.

Of course, it is often a necessary for simplicity to be compromised for more powerful functionality. There's a reason practically no professional YouTuber uses Apple iMovie to edit their videos: it is way too simple! It does not offer the functionality required to make stunning effects, because it strives to be simple.

The takeaway is: simplicity should be prioritized. To have more may come at the cost of simplicity - but you'd better try to minimize that cost as much as you can.

Terry A. Davis, the exceptionally talented programmer who created Temple OS, said, An idiot admires complexity, a genius admires simplicity. The point stands clear: Keep it simple, stupid!
