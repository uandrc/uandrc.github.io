How to Cook

Cooking fundamentals to become a competent cook.

  1. Learn food safety: proper storage and cooking temperatures, cross-contamination, etc.
  2. Learn how to deal with bad stuff: fires, cuts, burns, boilovers.
  3. Learn how to properly open hot pots and ovens to prevent steam and dry-heat burns.
  4. Learn how to safely move knives and hot cookware around the kitchen.
  5. Learn knife safety: proper cutting technique, ways to cut meat and produce.
  6. Learn to maintain a clean working area before, during, and after cooking.
  7. Learn to identify freshness, ripeness, and when food is spoiled.
  8. Learn proper measuring tools and technique for dry and liquid items.
  9. Familiarize yourself with core cooking tools: pan, pot, spatula, baking sheet.
  10. Learn the cooking techniques applicable to the aforementioned tools.
  11. Learn mise en place.
  12. Learn cooking timing of meats and vegetables.
  13. Develop substitution logic: knowing what ingredients you can swap for others.
  14. Learn science: Reduction, Maillard Reaction, Emulsification.
  15. Learn plating and food-presentation techniques.
  16. Follow a recipe you like. Mess it up. Repeat until it is palatable.
  17. Add obscene amounts of butter to make anything taste good
  18. Learn the roles of fat, sugar, salt, and carbohydrates. Understand how they balance each other.
